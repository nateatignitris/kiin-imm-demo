The philosophy

Five tastes,
one kitchen.

Some people are sweet, some are salty, and everybody knows someone who is a little spicy. Others might be considered downright bitter or sour. In the art of Thai street cuisine, those five fundamental tastes are balanced into each dish or the overall meal.

While Thai cooking may be known for its intense flavors, palates may be pleasantly surprised at the variety of refreshing flavors and aromas — ginger, coconut, lemongrass and kaffir lime — that give many of the dishes unique depth and complexity.

Kiin Imm Thai offers a unique menu reflecting the traditions of Thai street food cooking that still thrives in the Far East, from Grilled Pork Skewers with sticky rice and Thai Fish Cakes to sultry soups, fresh Thai salads and creamy curries.

Who are we

Eat until

"Kiin Imm" means "eat until full" in Thai — and that is our promise. Since 2018, every dish has been made from scratch with traditional recipes, fresh herbs, and the bold flavors of Thailand's street food culture.
